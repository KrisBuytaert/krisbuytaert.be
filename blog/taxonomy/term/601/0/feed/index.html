At first sight this doesn't look so bad , at second sight ..</p> <p>When weeks or months later the upstream project releases an urgent security fix, the local fork has deviated soo much that it can't upgrade anymore and stays with an insecure version.<br /> Often it's worse.. a feature that could have been accepted upstream has been implemented slightly different in the local fork, the result being that newer features depending on the first one also can't be integrated anymore </p> <p>Some projects are prepared for local contributions, they have a modular framework that allows you to build on top of the project while not having to touch the core of a project, Drupal and openQRM are great examples of those, but not all projects are that smart. Needless to say that when you have such a modular framework you really shouldn't be modifying the core part of the platform, unless you are fixing a real bug.</p> <p>But the general rule of thumb is that when you fix bugs, make sure they are inserted upstream , or implement new features.</p> <p>Now sometimes there is no easy way to get your code accepted upstream, in which case you should announce clearly that you want to contribute but you are blocked and publish the patches somewhere else ... </p> <p>Don't let the community work for you, but work with the community !</p> http://127.0.0.1:8080/blog/open-source-does-not-mean-customization-heaven#comments drupal jbilling mysql open source openqrm sugarcrm sun Wed, 11 Feb 2009 16:09:52 +0000 Kris Buytaert 878 at http://127.0.0.1:8080/blog openQRM.com http://127.0.0.1:8080/blog/openqrmcom <p><a href="http://virtualization.com/guest-posts/2008/11/16/openqrm-4.2-released-includes-cloud-sauce/" rel="nofollow">Last weekend</a> I blogged about openQRM 4.2 being released.</p> <p>This morning Matt finally let me know the long waiting fresh and new Drupal based <a href="http://openqrm.com/" rel="nofollow">openQRM.com</a> is live ! </p> <p>Feed Added!</p> http://127.0.0.1:8080/blog/openqrmcom#comments drupal openqrm virtualization.com Tue, 18 Nov 2008 08:27:17 +0000 Kris Buytaert 770 at http://127.0.0.1:8080/blog The Little 4 are back, John interviews Matt about openQRM, http://127.0.0.1:8080/blog/node/755 <p>I didn't even have time to finish my post about the Puppet Podcast and DevMinistration before John had already posted <a href="http://www.johnmwillis.com/cloudcafe/cloud-cafe-19-openqrm-the-little-four-are-back/" rel="nofollow">his chat</a> with <a href="http://mattinaction.blogspot.com/" rel="nofollow">Matt</a> as recorded yesterday in Eindhoven.</p> <p>I'm glad I could bring these guys together !
